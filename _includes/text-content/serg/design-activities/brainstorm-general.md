<p><span>ACTIVITY - Take Your Shoes Off!</span><br>
What’s a cultural or family practice that you didn’t realize was not the “norm” until later in life? What practices have you learned from others? Design a game that centers around narratives or mechanics of surprise, cultural exchange, and reactions or horror or delight.</p> 

<p><span>ACTIVITY - The Zombies are Anti-Capitalist!</span><br>
What’s your favorite monster, creature, or fantasy race from media? What metaphors have been explored? Which have not? Design a game engaging the interplay of metaphor and reality in fantasy.</p>

<p><span>ACTIVITY - Omiyage</span><br>
Think about a recent trip you went on. Consider omiyage, the Japanese tradition of bringing a gift home for a friend or loved one. Design a game that you would “bring back” inspired from your trip to “give to a friend.”</p>

<p><span>ACTIVITY - Osteomancy</span><br>
Grab some paper. Sketch a grid for placements on some of the spectrums that most excite you (e.g., a 2x2 grid for “meaningful mechanics” that says no / low / medium / high). For each spectrum you sketch, take a coin (or other trinket, runes, bones, etc.) and cast them onto the sketch to decide what placement you are aiming for. Design a game around the placements you divine.</p>

<p><span>ACTIVITY - Fusion* Mechanics</span><br>
Blend together two or more cultural influences into the mechanics (i.e., rules, win conditions, interactivity, etc.) of a game. </p>

<p><span>ACTIVITY - Resistencia*</span><br>
“Build around themes of resilience, survival, or standing up against oppression.”</p>

<p><span>ACTIVITY - Utopia</span><br>
Build a utopia. Where can diverse peoples of color live and thrive without racial violence and oppression? What does the friction of the game become? Build a game with heterogenous multiplicity of race and no acknowledgement of racism. How can race and racial and cultural differences still feel significant and meaningful? Can they?</p>
