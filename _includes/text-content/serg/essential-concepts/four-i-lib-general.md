<p><span>Ideological Liberation</span>: the idea of any system, collective or space being free of hierarchy and making sure all people contributing are equally valued regardless of where they are from and or who they are.  No one group or person has control over another.</p>

<p><span>Interpersonal Liberation</span>: the support and solidarity between all oppressed people.</p>

<p><span>Institutional Liberation</span>: recognizing that institutions reinforce oppressive systems and the push to dismantle them.</p>

<p><span>Internalized Liberation</span>: an individual's duty to reduce the importance and pressure of economic capital, need for hierarchy, and productivity by focusing on the main goal of liberation and acknowledgment that liberation is met through solidarity and not division.</p>
