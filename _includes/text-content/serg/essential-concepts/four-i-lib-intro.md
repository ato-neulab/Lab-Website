<p>As we elaborate on later in the Design for BIPOC Flourishing, recent discussions around race and ethnic studies highlight how discussions often center around trauma and harm; inspiring recent work focusing BIPOC liberation and flourishing. The 4 I’s of liberation were influenced by the 4 I’s of Oppression, however allows for conversations for liberating marginalized groups. Defined by Kiki Ochoa and Guillermo Gomez, liberation is “The struggle against dominance and oppression where people fight for and/or gain complete freedom from the systematic abuse, exploitation and injustice of the oppressor” (Ochoa and Gomez 2023).</p>
