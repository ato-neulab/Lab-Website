<p><span>Race is socially constructed</span>: race and racial differences are not biologically inherent but rather categories formed to enforce social hierarchies.</p>

<p><span>Differential Racialization</span>: the ruling class will racialize different minority groups at different times according to the needs of the labor market.</p>

<p><span>Racism is ordinary in our Socio-Technical World</span>: racism is not simply one off instances of explicit violence or harassment but rather embedded inextricably and sometimes invisibly into many aspects of our digital society and technological systems.</p>

<p><span>Interest Convergence/Material Determinism</span>: social progress will occur only insofar as it materially benefits whiteness.</p>

<p><span>Critique of Colorblindness</span>: a ‘colorblind’ approach to equality that mandates equal treatment across policies can only eliminate blatant forms of discrimination and does not eliminate inequality or inequity that has been established through continuous historical processes and events.</p>

<p><span>Tech Liberalism is Limiting</span>: “The academic, technocratic, and capitalist aversion for race conscious work inhibits the fight against racism".</p>

<p><span>Voice-of-Color Thesis</span>: the histories and experiences of People of Color enable them to communicate ideas and experiences of oppression that white people cannot speak to. BIPOC voices should be empowered to share their lived experiences.</p>

<p><span>Intersectionality</span>: identities such as race and gender are not discrete but are rather overlapping and compounding, resulting in particular lived experiences according to the intersection of identities.</p>
